Crowning Glory

A crowning glory is a phrase often used to describe the most distinctive, important, or successful part of something.

Examples

  • In ancient Greece, Olympians were crowned with "laurel wreaths" as a crowning glory for their achievements.
  • The crowning glory of a successful production is the performance that is given on the last night.
  • The crowning glory of a beautiful garden is the flowers that are in bloom.
  • The crowning glory of a delectable meal is the dessert.

Symbolism

The phrase has come to symbolize the highest achievement or the most desired goal. It is often used to describe a sense of accomplishment after something has been completed. The phrase is often used in a positive or celebratory context, but can sometimes be used to refer to something negative. For example, when a person has achieved a goal that seemed unattainable, the achievement may be deemed as the "crowning glory" of that person.

Origin

The phrase has been around for centuries and has roots in the Bible. In the Bible, the phrase is used to describe the utmost glory of God and is found in various passages throughout the Old and New Testaments. The phrase is also found in ancient Greek literature, where it is used to refer to the highest honor or achievement.

Conclusion

In modern usage, the phrase is used to describe the most important or successful part of something. It often refers to the highest degree of success, admiration, or glory. The phrase is often used to reflect a sense of achievement or accomplishment, whether it be in the form of a physical or mental accomplishment. Regardless of the context, the phrase "crowning glory" is often used to refer to the highest point of success or greatness.
